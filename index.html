This page is designed to help with revision for cyber security. The first part is a clear article-style overview. The second part embeds the original guide so you do not need to separately download the file on another device.

What the guide covers

The source pages focus on firewall types, the difference between hardware firewalls, software firewalls, and Firewall as a Service, plus how anti-virus, VPNs, and access control fit into a complete security setup.

It also covers legal and compliance points such as the Computer Misuse Act, the Fraud Act, and the need for extra controls like data loss prevention when firewall and anti-virus alone are not enough.

Core products and ideas

The material compares products such as Cisco ASA 1100, Cisco AMP, and Perimeter 81. It looks at price, strengths, weaknesses, support, and how well each option fits business needs. A big theme is that centralised security is usually stronger because firewall, anti-virus, and VPN can work together instead of being split across lots of separate tools.

For exam answers, do not just define the tool. Explain why it is chosen, what threat it deals with, and what its limitation is.

How to revise from this page
